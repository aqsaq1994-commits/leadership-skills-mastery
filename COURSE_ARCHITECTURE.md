# Leadership Skills Mastery: Course Architecture & Design Rationale

**Course Code:** LSM-2026 | **Target Audience:** Mixed (self-paced, modular) | **Duration:** 8–12 weeks (self-directed)

---

## 🎯 Course Thesis & Design Philosophy

This course is architected for **practicing professionals and emerging leaders** seeking structured, competency-based development in real-world leadership contexts. Each module combines conceptual frameworks (Situational Leadership, Emotional Intelligence, Change Management theory) with scenario-based learning and reflective practice.

**Design Principles:**
- **Modular & self-paced:** Learners progress at their speed; modules stand alone or sequence
- **Framework-grounded:** Every lesson anchors to established leadership models (Situational Leadership, Kouzes & Posner, Goleman EI)
- **Action-oriented:** Assessments include real-world application tasks, not just knowledge checks
- **Scalable & maintainable:** Markdown + React ensures easy updates and multi-platform delivery

---

## 📋 Module Sequence (8 Core Modules)

### **Module 1: Foundations of Leadership**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Remember, Understand, Apply)**
- Define leadership vs. management and situational leadership concepts
- Identify personal leadership style and core values
- Recognize how leadership context shapes approach

**Content Outline:**
- What is leadership? (Definition, myth-busting)
- Situational Leadership Model (Hersey & Blanchard) introduction
- Self-assessment: Leadership values & style inventory
- Real-world case: Leading in crisis vs. stable growth

**Interactive Component:** Leadership Style Self-Assessment (React: dynamic questionnaire → profile report)

**Assessment:** Reflection journal + style profile

**Kirkpatrick L1-L2:** Engagement (engaging questionnaire) + Learning (style profile validates concept retention)

---

### **Module 2: Emotional Intelligence & Self-Awareness**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Understand, Apply, Analyze)**
- Understand EI model (Goleman 5 domains)
- Assess your emotional intelligence strengths and gaps
- Apply self-awareness to leadership decision-making

**Content Outline:**
- Emotional Intelligence framework deep-dive
- The 5 EI competencies: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, social skills
- How EI predicts leadership effectiveness (research-backed)
- Scenario analysis: How EI shapes team dynamics

**Interactive Component:** EI Scenario Matcher (React: situation cards → match to EI competency + strategies)

**Assessment:** Case study reflection + EI development plan

**Kirkpatrick L3:** Behavior change opportunity (learner identifies one EI gap to work on)

---

### **Module 3: Building & Managing High-Performing Teams**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Understand, Apply, Analyze)**
- Apply team development models (Tuckman)
- Diagnose team performance issues
- Design interventions for team effectiveness

**Content Outline:**
- Team development stages: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing
- Psychological Safety & Belonging (Google's Project Aristotle)
- Conflict resolution frameworks (Thomas-Kilmann modes)
- Building trust: Concrete practices

**Interactive Component:** Team Diagnosis Tool (React: interactive flowchart matching team symptoms to interventions)

**Assessment:** Team challenge analysis + action plan

**Kirkpatrick L3:** Workplace application (learner applies framework to their actual team)

---

### **Module 4: Communication & Influence**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Apply, Analyze, Evaluate)**
- Master communication models (Active Listening, Non-Violent Communication)
- Diagnose communication breakdowns
- Influence without authority

**Content Outline:**
- Core communication skills: Active listening, asking powerful questions
- Crucial Conversations framework (Patterson et al.)
- Influence models: Cialdini, positional vs. personal power
- Difficult conversations: Feedback, performance, conflict

**Interactive Component:** Conversation Simulator (React: branching dialogue → real-time feedback on communication choices)

**Assessment:** Real conversation analysis + reframe exercise

**Kirkpatrick L2-L3:** Knowledge (understand frameworks) + Behavior opportunity (apply to actual conversation)

---

### **Module 5: Decision-Making & Problem-Solving**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Analyze, Evaluate, Create)**
- Apply decision-making frameworks (RACI, decision matrix)
- Recognize cognitive biases in leadership decisions
- Lead problem-solving in uncertainty

**Content Outline:**
- Decision-making models: Rational vs. intuitive
- Cognitive biases in leadership (confirmation bias, groupthink, availability heuristic)
- Frameworks: RACI matrix, decision trees, scenarios
- Leading in ambiguity: Agile & adaptive leadership

**Interactive Component:** Bias Detective (React: identify biases in case scenarios + reframe decisions)

**Assessment:** Complex case study with multiple right answers + decision rationale

**Kirkpatrick L3:** Critical thinking + application to ill-structured problems

---

### **Module 6: Change Leadership & Resilience**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Understand, Apply, Analyze)**
- Apply change management models (Kotter, Lewin)
- Build organizational resilience
- Lead people through change

**Content Outline:**
- Change is constant: VUCA environment framing
- Kotter's 8-step change model
- Lewin's 3-stage model (Unfreeze → Change → Refreeze)
- Resistance to change: Root causes & strategies
- Personal resilience + modeling resilience

**Interactive Component:** Change Scenario Planner (React: build a change roadmap, anticipate resistance, design communications)

**Assessment:** Change initiative design + stakeholder analysis

**Kirkpatrick L3-L4:** Behavior change + organizational impact potential

---

### **Module 7: Inclusive Leadership & Diversity**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Understand, Apply, Analyze)**
- Understand unconscious bias and inclusion
- Build psychological safety across diverse teams
- Lead with cultural intelligence

**Content Outline:**
- Diversity, equity, inclusion, belonging (DEIB) framework
- Unconscious bias: Recognition & mitigation
- Generational & cultural intelligence
- Allyship & sponsorship
- Creating belonging: Concrete practices

**Interactive Component:** Bias Scenario Analyzer (React: recognize bias in workplace situations + apply DEIB principles)

**Assessment:** Inclusive leadership self-audit + action plan

**Kirkpatrick L3:** Behavior change (commit to inclusion practice)

---

### **Module 8: Strategic Thinking & Vision**
**Learning Objectives (Bloom's: Analyze, Evaluate, Create)**
- Think strategically: Long-term vs. operational
- Craft & communicate vision
- Balance strategy with execution

**Content Outline:**
- Strategic thinking competencies: Systems thinking, foresight, adaptability
- Vision & purpose: Why they matter, how to craft them
- Translating strategy to action
- Balancing short-term wins with long-term vision
- Leading across organizational silos

**Interactive Component:** Vision Builder (React: interactive tool to articulate vision, validate it against strategy)

**Assessment:** Personal leadership strategy + 90-day action plan

**Kirkpatrick L3-L4:** Personal commitment + organizational relevance

## 📊 Assessment & Evaluation Strategy (Kirkpatrick Model)

| Level | Measure | Delivery |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **L1: Reaction** | Engagement, perceived relevance, UX quality | Post-module surveys (embedded) |
| **L2: Learning** | Knowledge checks, scenario analysis, self-assessments | Interactive modules, quizzes, reflection journals |
| **L3: Behavior** | Application to real scenarios, action plans, peer/manager feedback | Case studies, workplace application tasks, reflections |
| **L4: Results** | Career progression, team effectiveness, organizational impact | Portfolio case study (learner documents progress) |

## 🏗️ Technical Architecture

- **Content Delivery:** Markdown lessons (GitHub native) + Interactive React components
- **Hosting:** GitHub Pages (free, professional)
- **Interactivity:** React modules for scenarios, assessments, planning tools
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (keyboard nav, color contrast, semantic HTML)
- **Version Control:** Git-based for iteration and portfolio demonstration
